Gerund: Verb with 'ing'

Infinitive : TO run

Base form: run


Preposition: Article that expresses something 'in', 'an'

## The use of the gerund
1. After prepositions: e.g, I'm  good *at* **talking** to people
2. After certain verbs: don't mind, feel like, avoid, quit, etc. E.g. 'I *suggest* **talking** to Chris first'

## The use of infinitive
1. After adjectives; E.g., 'It's *interesting* **to talk** to James
2. After WH words; E.g. 'I don't know *how* **to behave** 
3. When you give a reason
   I organized everything *to be* **more collaborative**
4. After objects
   They asked *me* **to work**
5. After certain verbs

### Other uses
1. to state a fact or an opinion with an *impersonal*it. Impersonal it refers to the use of the pronouns 'it' as the subject when the subject is not a specfic person or thinf

## The use of both
1. As nouns (subject or objects)
   *Meeting/To meet* new people is hard
   My hobby is *going/ going to* camping
2. After the same verbs or verb phrases, in both cases having the same meaning
   start, prefer
   I **prefer** *meeting/ yo meet* outgoing people

## The use of the Base form
1. After modal verbs (can, must, should, may, might, etc.
2. After auxiliare verbs
3. After LET, MAKE and HAVE, we also have an object but it doesn't affect
